## What?

Linguistics is the scientific study of languages. This includes phonology: how humans quantize sounds into discrete units, syntax: how words are connected to build a larger structure, semantics: the relation between meaning and language. 

Most of my work has been in syntax, though currently I am taking a phonology course (LING321) and I am quite excited to do some research here as well.

## Computer Science 

What fascinates me the most about it is how many parallels it draws from computer science.   

For example, how computer language compilers lexicalize words, parse them into sentences, and then evaluate their meaning. There is some philosophical questions to be asked about if its inherently human to organize information into language-like structures. I will not be asking these questions right now. 

I would highly recommend taking CMSC330 or CMSC430 alongside LING311 (or equivalent in other colleges) to fully explore this overlap.
