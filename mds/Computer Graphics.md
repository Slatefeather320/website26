In early 2020 (remember Covid?), I, like many other people, found myself with a lot of time on my hands. 

One of the hobbies I used that time for is 3D modeling and rendering. Specifically, I discovered the open source 3D creation suite [Blender](https://www.blender.org/)

You can find a lot of the work I made during this time on [this Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/slatefeather_renders/) account.

***

A lot of what I have done since was directly influenced by this happenstance Covid hobby. 

- It was the catalyst for me wanting to make a portfolio website to showcase all the art. This taught me the basics of UI design and web programming. (and what a nightmare DNS records are). This website no longer exists as my interests moved on but ~most of the artwork I'm proud of is still on the Instagram. 
- It got me interested in Physically Based Rendering techniques. Here are some excellent YouTube channels which cover topics in PBR: [Acerola](https://www.youtube.com/c/Acerola_t), [Sebastian Lague](https://www.youtube.com/@SebastianLague). 
- As an extension to that, I am currently taking CMSC427 Computer Graphics where I am learning WebGL. This course focuses on the lower level theoretical aspects (its matrices and dot products ALL the way through). 
- I was invited by [IIT BHU](https://iitbhu.ac.in/) to participate in Film and Media Council's Digital Design competition where I met quite a few people interested in similar things.
- I started posting Blender tutorials on my YouTube channel, one of which currently has 11K views.

## Technical Renders

In November 2022, I interned at [Amantaya Technologies](https://www.amantyatech.com/) where I used blender to make 3d Models of their products (5G base stations) from schematics, promotional image mock ups, and animations for internal use. 

Later when I was working at Sharang Shakti (read more about that in [[Flight Control Systems]]), I was also making technical renders for them for project proposals.
